--- title: Short Bio layout: default bio: Thomas (TJ) Vandal recently defended his PhD in Interdisciplinary Engineering as part of the the SDS Lab at Northeastern University under the supervision of Auroop Ganguly and Evan Kodra. During his PhD, TJ won multiple awards including a runner-up best paper at KDD 2017 and best dissertation in Civil and Environmental Engineering. In August 2018 TJ will join the NASA Earth Exchange at Ames Research Center as a Research Scientist for the Bay Area Environmental Research Intitute. His research is interdiscplinary in the areas of machine learning, bayesian deep learning, climate, and remote sensing. Currently, he is interested in developing uncertainty aware high-resolution datasets in the earth sciences with methods typically applied to computer vision and image processing problems. Prior to his graduate studies, he worked as a Data Scientist at Affectiva building large-scale tools to analyze facial expressions. He also worked as a Market Risk Analyst at Boston Technologies managing millions in exposure to the foreign exchange markets. He also holds a BS in Mathematics from the University of Maryland. --- {% include header.html %}

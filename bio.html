BIO: Thomas (TJ) Vandal recently defended his PhD in Interdisciplinary Engineering as part of the the SDS Lab at Northeastern University under the supervision of Auroop Ganguly and Evan Kodra. During his PhD, TJ won multiple awards including a runner-up best paper at KDD 2017 and best dissertation in Civil and Environmental Engineering. In August TJ will join the NASA Earth Exchange at Ames Research Center as a Research Scientist for the Bay Area Environmental Research Intitute. His research is focused in the intersection of machine learning and the earth sciences. Specifically, around the area of image super-resolution applied to low-resolution climate variables and satellite imagery. Prior to his graduate studies, he worked as a Data Scientist at Affectiva building large-scale tools to analyze facial expressions. He also worked as a Market Risk Analyst at Boston Technologies managing millions in exposure to the foreign exchange markets. His undergraduate studies in mathematics was completed at the University of Maryland, Go Terps!
